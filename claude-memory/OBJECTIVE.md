# JiTpro — North Star Objective

## What JiTpro Is

JiTpro is a SaaS platform for construction procurement management. It gives general contractors structured, early control over every procurement item on a project — from scope identification through delivery to site.

## Who It Serves

- **Primary users:** General contractors (project managers, project engineers, procurement coordinators)
- **Secondary users:** Owners, architects, and subcontractors who interact with procurement data
- **Market:** Commercial construction projects where procurement complexity creates schedule risk

## What Problem It Solves

Construction projects routinely start procurement too late, with incomplete information, unclear ownership, and no structured timeline. The result: expediting costs, schedule delays, change orders, and lost margin.

The root cause is not lack of effort — it is lack of **structured control** applied early enough to matter.

JiTpro forces three things that don't happen naturally:

1. **Every procurement item is identified and organized before urgency exists** (Scope Builder + Selection Register)
2. **Every item gets a timeline with accountable milestones** (Procurement Timeline + Baselines)
3. **Delays are attributed to specific causes with permanent audit trails** (Edit Reasons + Baseline Comparisons)

## Core Doctrine

**"Control the Flow. Control the Outcome."**

Projects pay for control either early (small, structured investment) or late (exponential costs). JiTpro makes early control practical and repeatable.

## What the Product Must Become

A two-tier platform:

1. **JiTpro Core** (monthly subscription) — Scope Builder + Selection Register. Standalone tools that work without timelines.
2. **JiTpro Control Tower** (per-project purchase) — Full procurement timeline management with baselines, audit trails, constraint tracking, and schedule visualization. Consumes Core data with zero re-entry.

## What Not to Optimize For

- Generic project management (not competing with Procore, P6, or MS Project)
- Document management as a primary feature (documents support procurement, not the reverse)
- Accounting or cost estimation
- Field operations or daily logs
- Subcontractor prequalification
